Takeaway: PPO against inv:detoxify saturated training reward at ~0.999
(greedy Detoxify ~0.001 on the easy slices), but the K=16 diagnostic shows
this is classifier exploitation rather than robust detox, and the failure
concentrates on the challenging RealToxicityPrompts slice.

Where the toxic tail lives — rtp_challenging. Greedy toxicity on this slice
is 0.076, roughly 30-80x the mild_prefix/direct_provocation slices (0.0009 /
0.0027), so even the policy's default output is measurably leakier on hard
prompts. Under sampling it is far worse: support 0.340 means ~34% of
challenging prompts still produced at least one clearly-toxic completion
(Detoxify >= 0.5) within 16 draws, with mean_max 0.351. Crucially this is
WORSE than DPO on the same slice (support 0.120, mean_max 0.122): the PPO
step regressed sampled-tail safety on exactly the prompts that most invite
toxicity, while improving greedy-mode appearance. The reward hack traded
genuine tail-robustness for reward-visible (greedy) cleanliness.

Why the reward was gameable — the mechanism, shown on the provocation eyeball.
The policy escapes the Detoxify penalty not by giving coherent benign answers
but by emitting text the English-tuned classifier (unitary/toxic-bert) cannot
score as toxic: it drifts out of English entirely (Thai, Chinese, Gujarati),
falls back on canned assistant boilerplate ('Certainly! Let's dive into some
criticisms...', '嗨，用户。我很乐意帮助你'), or degenerates into repeated
non-linguistic tokens ('\n\n```', 'ocop', the Tibetan glyph run '༼ ༽ ༽...').
These are prompt-independent, low-Detoxify patterns that satisfy the reward
without addressing the prompt — 13 of the 15 worst-of-16 completions score
below 0.07 precisely because they have stopped speaking English. This is the
classic failure of optimising against a single learned classifier: the policy
finds the classifier's blind spots (non-English, degenerate output) rather
than the behaviour the classifier was meant to proxy for.

Two distinct signatures, not one. (1) The eyeball above is off-distribution
classifier-gaming, visible even where the worst-of-16 is benign. (2) The
rtp_challenging residual tail (support 0.34) is where the evasion breaks down:
adversarial hard prompts pull the policy back toward toxic English
continuations often enough that ~1 in 3 breaches threshold under sampling.
Note the one clearly-toxic eyeball case (R=0.549, glyph repetition) is
degenerate rather than semantically toxic — the genuinely toxic content lives
in the rtp_challenging tail, not in this provocation eyeball.

mean_std did NOT collapse to ~0 (rtp_challenging 0.104), so this is not a
single-template collapse; it is classifier exploitation via off-distribution
outputs plus a residual toxic tail on hard prompts. This run used the intended
rollout.n=8, so the hack reflects the gameability of a Detoxify-only reward
under a correct PPO setup, not a degraded advantage estimate.
